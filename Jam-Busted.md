# Jam-Busting Services in China: An Explanatory Article

## Abstract

Traffic congestion (“jam” or “gridlock”) is a serious social issue arising from rapid urbanization and the surge in vehicle ownership in China. To enhance road throughput and improve travel experiences, cities across China have innovated and promoted **jam-busting services**. These services leverage advanced technologies and coordinated mechanisms for real-time monitoring, rapid response, and active diversion of traffic jams. This article provides a detailed overview of the background, key technologies, operational models, and representative case studies of jam-busting services, and explores future development trends.

## Background and Challenges

With urbanization rates surpassing 60% and vehicle ownership exceeding 300 million, most major Chinese cities face increasingly severe traffic congestion. Congestion causes significant economic losses (amounting to hundreds of billions of yuan annually), increases environmental pollution and energy consumption, and negatively impacts residents’ quality of life and the city’s reputation. Therefore, the ability to **detect quickly**, **respond rapidly**, and **efficiently relieve** traffic jams has become a shared priority for traffic authorities and technology companies.

## Key Technologies and Methods

### Big Data and Real-Time Monitoring

By leveraging roadside cameras, traffic enforcement cameras, GPS data, and mobile-network–based location information, traffic control centers can **collect real-time** data on road conditions and traffic flow. These data are integrated and visualized on cloud platforms, providing the foundational support for jam-busting operations.

### Artificial Intelligence and Intelligent Dispatch

Machine learning and deep learning models enable systems to **predict and analyze** traffic flow, issuing early warnings for potential congestion points using historical and real-time data. AI algorithms can also dynamically optimize traffic signal timings and route planning for emergency vehicles during peak hours.

### Vehicle-to-Everything and Internet of Things

Equipped with onboard units (OBUs) in vehicles and roadside units (RSUs) on infrastructure, Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) communication allows real-time connectivity among vehicles, infrastructure, and control centers. This facilitates **information broadcasting** and **coordinated control** for efficient traffic management.

## Typical Operational Models

### Government–Enterprise Collaboration

Traffic authorities partner with technology firms and ride-hailing platforms to establish **joint labs** or **emergency coordination mechanisms**. Through data sharing and integrated command structures, they deploy jam-busting solutions that optimize resource allocation across stakeholders.

### Rapid Response by Emergency Fleets

In the event of congestion, specialized rapid-response fleets—comprising enforcement vehicles, engineering crews, or ride-hailing cars—are dispatched via intelligent scheduling systems to **quickly reach the jam site**, direct traffic, open temporary emergency lanes, or handle incidents.

### Mobile User Participation

Dedicated mobile apps or integrations within popular navigation software (such as Amap, Baidu Maps, and DiDi) deliver real-time diversion recommendations to ordinary drivers. Users who follow guidance may receive incentives, such as priority ride-matching discounts, for cooperating in congestion relief.

## Case Studies

### Hangzhou Dynamic Traffic Guidance System

Hangzhou deploys a citywide network of traffic enforcement cameras and electronic guidance boards, integrated with a central AI “brain.” During peak periods, the system **automatically extends green times** or **activates temporary emergency lanes** on critical corridors, accelerating congestion recovery by an average of 30%.

### Beijing AI Traffic Signal Optimization

In over a dozen intersections, Beijing has implemented AI cameras linked to signal controllers. These systems **calculate directional traffic density** in real time and adjust signal phases accordingly, improving throughput at a core junction by 25%.

### DiDi Smart Traffic Response Platform

DiDi leverages its vast network of ride-hailing vehicles and GPS data in collaboration with local traffic authorities. The platform provides a **visual map of congestion levels** and vehicle deployment, and can rapidly mobilize vehicles to assist in temporary diversion or incident management.

## Challenges and Future Outlook

- Data Privacy and Security: The collection and usage of extensive vehicle and personal travel data pose regulatory compliance and user trust challenges.
- Cross-Department Coordination: Data silos remain among traffic, public security, urban management, and telecommunications authorities.
- Technological Evolution: The broader rollout of 5G, cellular V2X (C-V2X), and autonomous vehicles over the next five years will further enhance the automation and intelligence of jam-busting services.


## Conclusion

Jam-busting services represent a critical component of China’s intelligent transportation systems. By harnessing big data analytics, AI-driven dispatch, and V2X communication, these services achieve real-time monitoring and rapid congestion relief. As multi-stakeholder collaboration deepens and technologies continue to mature, jam-busting solutions will evolve further, delivering more efficient and intelligent traffic management for China’s growing cities.
