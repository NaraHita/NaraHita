# How to Open a New PowerPoint File

This document is a brief step-by-step turotial on opening and editing a new PowerPoint File for new users who have no familiarity with computers.

Briefly, if someone gave you a powerpoint and you don't know what is a powerpoint, this document is for you.

A _PowerPoint_ presentation is a series of digital slides created with *Microsoft PowerPoint* to visually present information. Follow these simple steps to create and open a brand-new PowerPoint presentation, even if you have very little experience with computers.

## 1. Turn on Your Computer 
1. Press the power button on your desktop tower or laptop.  
2. Wait until Windows or macOS loads and you see the desktop screen.

## 2. Locate Microsoft PowerPoint
- **On Windows:**  
  1. Click the **Start** button (Windows logo) at the bottom-left corner.  
  2. In the menu that appears, scroll or type **PowerPoint**.  
  3. Click **Microsoft PowerPoint** in the search results.

- **On Mac:**  
  1. Click the **Finder** icon (smiley face) in the Dock at the bottom.  
  2. Click **Applications** in the sidebar.  
  3. Double-click **Microsoft PowerPoint**.

## 3. Create a New Presentation
1. Once PowerPoint opens, you’ll see a **Welcome** or **Home** screen.  
2. Look for and click the button or thumbnail labeled **Blank Presentation** or **New**.  
   - This creates a fresh, empty slide deck.  
3. Optionally, you can choose a **Template** instead of a blank file by clicking one of the template thumbnails (e.g., “Modern,” “Business,” “Simple”).

## 4. Verify Your New File Is Open
- You should now see:  
  - A large white slide area in the center.  
  - A list of slides on the left side (initially only one slide).  
  - Toolbars and menus at the top.

- The title bar at the very top usually says **“Presentation1 – PowerPoint”** (or a similar default name).

## 5. Save Your New Presentation
1. Click **File** in the top-left corner.  
2. Select **Save As** (Windows) or **Save** (Mac).  
3. Choose where to save (e.g., **Documents**).  
4. Type a name for your file (e.g., “My First Presentation”).  
5. Click **Save**.

Congratulations! You’ve successfully opened a new PowerPoint file and saved it. You’re ready to start adding content to your slides.
